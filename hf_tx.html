Healthy Futures
of Texas

Website Redesign

Role
  • User Research
  • Insights & Analysis
  • UI Design
  • Wireframe & Prototype
  • Front End Development
For this website redesign project, I contributed to the persona creation, sitemap design and user testing. I worked exclusively on the UI design, wireframing, prototype and front end development.
Overview
Healthy Futures of Texas is a non-profit in San Antonio, Texas that develops science-based sexual health education and advocates for state funding and policies to ensure Texas women have access to preventative healthcare.
Upon review of the their website we uncovered some heuristic, navigation issues and uncovered that all user submitted contact request are routed through one person at the agency, and at times, can become a bottleneck.
Our solution was to redesign the Contact Us form, clean up the navigation and re-organize the content on their homepage.
Timeline
We had 3-weeks to complete and present our redesigned website.
Process
Following the double diamond design thinking process allowed us to stay flexible, collaborate and iterate on the project.
User Persona
In researching the website, I found that our initial proto-persona could be a potential user however the main user would be a person who would implement sex ed in schools and communities. This lead me to create a new persona which we shared with the stakeholder.
Stakeholder Interview
During our interview with the stakeholder we found that our iterated persona was spot on with their typical user and uncovered that all user submitted contact request were routed through him and at times become a bottleneck.
Card Sorting & Site Map
To get a better picture of the site’s navigation, we conducted a card sorting exercise and created a site map.
UI Design System
As my partners were working through their task, I took the initiative to design the UI system for the site.
I decided to keep the brand intact, redesign some of the iconography and button states and streamline the typography hierarchy.
Lo-Fidelity Wireframe
We began designing our wireframes with a mobile first approach. As a group we divided the wireframing amongst ourselves. I designed the homepage, navigation menu and donation page.
Wireframe Iteration
After the initial donation wireframe design, I noticed that the user is given two donation options however both feature the same experience and capabilities.
I decided to reduce the redundancy, and possible confusion, and provided the user with one option and reorganized the flow of the content.
User Testing
I was able to conduct 2 out of 5 user tests for the group and received some good feedback.
Key Takeaways
  • Flow was intuitive
  • Suggested drop down box on Contact Form
  • Ability to select a program of interest on the Contact Form
User Feedback Iterations
Based on feedback from user testing, I implemented the drop down boxes and functionality of selecting a program of interest on the Contact Form.
Interactive Prototype
I gathered my groups pages and built out the clickable, hi-fidelity prototype in Figma.

Front-End Development
Using Visual Studio Code and GitHub, I was able to put my knowledge of HTML, Bootstrap, CSS and JavaScript to work and code out our website prototype.

Results and Takeaways
We presented the hi-fidelity prototype to the Healthy Futures of Texas stakeholder and recieved their approval. Looking back, it amazes me how much work we collectively put into this redesign in a short amount of time. This project really illustrated the importance of conducting an stakeholder interview and getting usability feedback. It also reminded me to take a step back and place myself in the user’s shoes.
  • Gaining insight from a stakeholder helps the process
  • User feedback can better shape the solution
  • Remember to take a step back and place myself in the user's shoes
Next Steps
  • Add micro interactions to the site to keep the user engaged
  • Finalize the front end development of the prototype
  • Continue dialogue with the stakeholder for any future needs
