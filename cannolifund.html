The Cannoli Fund

Website Redesign

Role
  • User Research
  • Insights & Analysis
  • User Flow
  • UI Design
  • Wireframe & Prototype
In this project, I planned and conducted the interview with the founder of The Cannoli Fund, distilled the findings into an affinity diagram, developed a user flow for the prioritized feature of the website, contributed to the UI style guide, created lo-fi and hi-fi wireframes and prototypes.
Overview
Our bootcamp class was split into groups and given a project to select a local nonprofit and redesign their website. The Cannoli Fund is a nonprofit that operates in two neighborhoods south of downtown San Antonio, Texas specializing in reducing the feral cat population in the neighborhood. Their website was designed using a WordPress template that is no longer supported, causing usability issues on the their site.
The goal of the redesign was to identify and correct usability issues, and determine a feature to prioritize.
Timeline
We were given 3-weeks to complete and present the project.
Process
We followed the double diamond design thinking process which allowed us to stay flexible, collaborate and iterate on the project.
Stakeholder Interview
In this research project, I planned and conducted a stakeholder interview with the founder of The Cannoli Fund—then distilled the information into an affinity diagram.
The interview plan was designed to:
Better understand the services provided
Uncover the goals and pain points of their website
Uncover the goals and pain points of their organization
Key Takeaways
  • Header and footer navigation need updating; unsupported web template
  • Need to organize the services offered
  • Need to create a mobile version of the website
  • Funding is received from local and state grants; not through donations
User Flow
Through our research we determined we would focus on the donation feature. I began designing the user flow by placing myself in the user’s shoes and mapping out a process.
Insights from usability testing, stakeholder feedback and researching other nonprofits donation process assisted in iterating the initial user flow.
Lo-Fidelity Wireframe
Started with building the mobile low-fidelity wireframes in Figma, using the sitemap created by a team member as the guide for the updated navigation and the user flow for the donation feature.
Home Screen Iteration
Main takeaways from an initial group feedback included, the Services and News sections looked too similar and appeared to have only 1 item in each section. To solve for this, I decided to create a better visual hierarchy for the sections by adding a new font for the section titles and enlarging the font size. Then by adding a background color to the Services section and breaking the content of each section into smaller chunks further aided the visual hierarchy.
Navigation Iteration
For the main navigation menu, adding the new font created a visual hierarchy between the primary, secondary and footer links. This also allowed me to clean up the secondary links in the dropdown menu by removing the icons. Adding the footer and social links into the main navigation menu was another takeaway from the initial group feedback.
Donation Feature Iteration
A key takeaway from usability testing was adding an anonymous donation feature and I began researching other non-profits to see their donation experience. In doing so, I developed a solution for the anonymous donation feature, added an “Other Ways to Donate” section that was overlooked and added options to the Donation Frequency feature.
UI Style Tile
As other team members conducted usability testing on the mobile prototype, I began researching icons for The Cannoli Fund services and researching images for the carousel. I also added a new color, navy, to The Cannoli Fund color palette to add contrast to the website.
Interactive Prototype
I created final hi-fidelity wireframes and prototype for both mobile and desktop in Figma. In creating the micro interactions, I wanted to keep them subtle yet playful like cats The Cannoli Fund helps.

Results and Takeaways
We presented the hi-fidelity prototype to The Cannoli Fund’s founder and recieved their approval. Overall this project was good experience in working with a stakeholder and the constraints of an established organization and brand.
  • Teamwork makes dreamwork. Splitting up the work allowed us to be flexible and stay on schedule.
  • Collaborating generates ideas. Bouncing ideas off each other leads to solutions especially when you get stuck on task.
  • User testing is important. Getting user feedback helps validate your solution and provides you with insights to iterate from.
